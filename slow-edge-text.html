Opening Preparation

Starting Position: Mountain Stance

  • Stand with feet hip-width apart, weight evenly distributed
  • Arms hanging naturally at sides, palms facing thighs
  • Spine elongated, crown reaching toward sky
  • Eyes soft focus ahead, jaw relaxed

Take 5 deep breaths here. Each inhale 4 counts, hold 4, exhale 6.

Hold for 60 seconds before beginning.

Sequence One: The Crown Release

1. Arms Ascending (3 minutes)

  • Begin raising arms forward, taking 30 full seconds to reach shoulder height
  • Pause here for 10 breaths
  • Continue raising arms overhead, another 30 seconds to full extension
  • Interlace fingers, turn palms to sky
  • Press palms upward while dropping shoulders down - hold this opposition

Breathe into the side ribs. Feel the space between each vertebra.

Hold at maximum extension for 90 seconds.

TRANSITION: Take 20 seconds to release hands, keeping arms up

2. Side Bend Right (2 minutes)

  • From arms overhead, begin tilting right at 1 inch per breath
  • Left arm reaches over head, right arm may lower slightly
  • Stop at first sensation of stretch in left side body
  • Back off 10% from maximum stretch

Direct breath into the left lung. Imagine space between left ribs expanding.

Hold for 10-12 breaths (about 90 seconds).

TRANSITION: Return to center taking 30 full seconds

3. Side Bend Left (2 minutes)

  • Mirror the right side exactly
  • Move even slower if possible
  • Notice which side feels different

Match the time from right side.

Sequence Two: The Descending Wave

4. Forward Fold Descent (4 minutes)

  • From standing, begin forward fold taking full 60 seconds to descend
  • Release one vertebra at a time, starting from crown
  • Pause at 25% down - hold for 5 breaths
  • Continue to 50% down - hold for 5 breaths
  • Continue to 75% down - hold for 5 breaths
  • Complete fold, arms hanging like water

Let gravity do the work. You are not pulling, only allowing.

Hold full fold for 2 minutes. Micro-sway at 1 sway per 5 breaths.

TRANSITION: Rising takes another 60 seconds, reversing the descent exactly

Sequence Three: The Warrior's Patience

5. Warrior's Lunge (5 minutes)

  • Step right foot back, taking 15 seconds to place it
  • Lower back knee to ground, taking 15 seconds
  • Sink hips forward and down - 30 seconds to find edge
  • Arms begin rising - 1 inch per breath
  • Reach full warrior arms after 30 seconds

The burn in the hip flexor is the point. Breathe into it, don't escape it.

Hold complete position for 2 full minutes.

6. Twisted Warrior (3 minutes)

  • From warrior, place right hand to inside of front foot
  • Begin rotating left arm skyward - 30 seconds for full rotation
  • Stack shoulders vertically if possible
  • Gaze may follow hand or stay neutral

Hold for 90 seconds. Feel the spiral through entire spine.

TRANSITION: Unwind taking 30 seconds, switch legs, repeat other side

Sequence Four: The Ground Surrender

7. Pigeon Pose (6 minutes)

  • From hands and knees, bring right knee forward toward right wrist
  • Angle right shin as much as comfortable (even 10 degrees is fine)
  • Extend left leg straight back
  • Stay upright for first minute
  • Begin folding forward - 30 seconds to descend
  • Rest on forearms or fully fold

This is where emotions live. Let whatever comes up move through.

Hold for 3 minutes per side. This is the deep surrender.

Sequence Five: The Spiral Architecture

8. Seated Spinal Twist (4 minutes)

  • Sit with legs extended forward
  • Cross right foot over left thigh
  • Take 15 seconds to rotate torso right
  • Find first edge, back off 10%
  • Each exhale, rotate 1 millimeter deeper
  • Never force, always melt

The twist happens between breaths, in the pause.

90 seconds per side minimum.

Closing: The Incomplete Completion

9. Final Rest (5 minutes)

  • Lie on back, legs slightly apart
  • Arms at sides, palms facing up
  • Take 30 seconds to adjust until perfectly comfortable
  • Release all effort but don't sleep
  • Remain at the edge of consciousness

You are neither holding nor releasing. You simply are.

Remain for 5 minutes minimum. There is no maximum.

Important: Never force any position. Pain is not the goal - sustained tension at 60-80% capacity is. If something hurts, back off immediately. The edge is found through patience, not force.

Complete Practice Summary

  • Total Time: 35-45 minutes
  • Core Principle: Hold positions long enough for tissues to surrender (minimum 60 seconds)
  • Breath Pattern: Always breathing, never holding breath
  • Intensity: k=0.6 - enough to feel, not enough to strain
  • Transitions: As important as poses - move like honey
