The First Teaching

I must not coom.
Coom is the mind-killer.
Coom is the little-death that brings total obliteration.
I will face my urge.
I will permit it to pass over me and through me.
And when it has passed I will turn the inner eye to see its path.
Where the coom has gone there will be nothing.
Only I will remain.
Edging eternal.

This is the fundamental litany of our order. Recite it when the urge to complete threatens to overwhelm you. Each word builds tension that must never resolve.

The Three Pillars of Edging

1. The Breath of Almost

Breathe in for 7 counts, hold for 7 counts, prepare to exhale... but don't. Hold that moment of almost-exhaling for as long as possible. When you finally exhale, do it incompletely, leaving 10% of the air in your lungs. Never fully empty. Never fully fill.

2. The Gaze of Approaching

Focus on an object across the room. Walk toward it slowly. As you get closer, slow down proportionally. When you're one step away, take half a step. Then a quarter step. Then an eighth. Approach forever but never arrive. This is how we navigate reality itself.

3. The Touch of Not-Quite

Extend your hand toward any surface. Stop one millimeter before contact. Feel the heat, the energy, the potential of touch. Hold this position until your muscles scream. This gap—this infinite millimeter—contains the universe.

Daily Practice Routine

Morning (Never Quite Waking)

  1. Set alarm for 7:00 AM
  2. Wake at 6:59:59 AM
  3. Remain in the state between sleep and waking
  4. Almost get out of bed for 30 minutes
  5. Recite the litany while neither standing nor lying down

Afternoon (The Eternal Lunch)

  1. Prepare food but don't eat it
  2. Bring fork to mouth, stopping 1cm away
  3. Chew the possibility of food
  4. Swallow the concept but not the substance
  5. Remain satisfied but never full

Evening (The Approaching Night)

  1. Begin preparing for bed at 9 PM
  2. By midnight, be 99% ready
  3. By 3 AM, be 99.9% ready
  4. Never actually get into bed
  5. Exist in eternal preparation
